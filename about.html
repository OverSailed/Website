About OverSailed

OverSailed is a small sailing project created by people who want to repair, sail, document and share the process. It connects classic sailing, boat restoration, engineering, electronics, photography and film.

What we do

Sailing

We sail, train, test ideas on the water and document what actually works outside of theory and marketing.

Restoration

One of the core parts of the project is the renovation of an old Swedish sailing yacht, S/Y Gracil.

Documentation

We want to show the process honestly: repairs, mistakes, costs, technical decisions and lessons learned.

Why OverSailed exists

The project started from a simple idea: sailing should not be only about finished yachts, perfect photos and expensive equipment. The process matters too.

We are interested in boats as real technical systems — hulls, engines, electronics, rigging, navigation, energy systems and all the small decisions that make a vessel reliable.

At the same time, we want to keep the human side visible: people, stories, travel, photography, film and nights spent under the sky, on deck or near water.

Main areas

S/Y Gracil renovation

Renovation of an old Swedish sailing yacht. The project includes hull work, onboard systems, safety, electrical installation and preparation for sailing.

TrådJacht

A technical project focused on autonomous sailing and low-cost, emission-free maritime transport concepts.

How we work

Practical first

We prefer solutions that can be tested, repaired and understood, not only described nicely.

Open process

We want to publish progress, photos, notes, technical choices and project files whenever possible.

Small crew

OverSailed is built by a small team, so the website is designed to grow slowly: one page, one project and one story at a time.
